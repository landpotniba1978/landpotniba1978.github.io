Font Categories

First, a quick lesson on fonts. There are two main font styles: serif and sans serif.

  1. A serif font has short lines at the ends of the letters, making it look less blocky, one of the most popular serif fonts that you might know is Times.
  2. Sans serif fonts are just that–a font “sans” or without serif. A common sans serif font is Arial.

There are also other font categories like script, handwritten, greek, and decorative fonts, which you’ll see in some of our examples below and categorized in our lab.

Pairing Fonts

An important design element for custom gear is pairing multiple fonts. Finding two unique fonts that work together will make your design look clean and professional. You’ll see in our graphic below some of our favorite sports fonts to pair together.

For a classic sports look, pair College B with Veneer Solid. The blockiness of College B will remind you of traditional sports apparel, and Veneer Solid works with it to give it a modern and trendy look. Consider using College B for your team’s name and Veneer Solid for your subhead such as “women’s league” or “intramural soccer.”

Playing a less traditional sport like broom ball or ultimate frisbee? Give your uniforms a one of a kind look by pairing Bullet Small with Bank Gothic. Bullet Small has a futuristic vibe with varying letters in uppercase and lowercase. Because it’s so unique, a modern but simple sans serif font like Bank Gothic is the perfect complement for your design.
